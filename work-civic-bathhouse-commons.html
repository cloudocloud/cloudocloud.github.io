Project / Culture + Housing

Civic Bathhouse Commons

Location

Copenhagen, Denmark

Timeline

2025

Focus

Shared care spaces, neighborhood rituals, and intergenerational living

Civic Bathhouse Commons combines affordable urban housing with a contemporary bathhouse and community rooms, proposing care infrastructure as a daily civic anchor rather than a niche program.

Idea

The brief asked how a housing-led development could contribute something more generous to the neighborhood. The response was to place bathing, recovery, small-scale exercise, and communal kitchens on the lower levels, giving the block a public rhythm beyond residential use alone.

Rather than separating social and domestic life, the project explores the threshold between them. Shared spaces are visible from the street and intentionally warm in tone, making entry feel open rather than institutional.

Spatial organization

Public rooms line the base of the building, while upper floors step back into quieter residential terraces. Circulation is layered so residents and visitors can share a sense of welcome without compromising privacy.

Materials are chosen to support a calm, durable atmosphere: tile, timber, mineral plaster, and muted lighting that makes the building feel closer to a public living room than a facility.

Why it matters

The proposal expands what housing can give back to a neighborhood. Instead of only adding units, it adds rituals: meeting after work, warming up in winter, and spending time together without needing to consume.

That social generosity is the central measure of the project. Density is still present, but it is made legible through comfort and collective use.
