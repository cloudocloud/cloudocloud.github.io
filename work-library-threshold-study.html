Project / Culture + Housing

Library Threshold Study

Location

Rotterdam, Netherlands

Timeline

2023

Focus

Welcoming entrances, soft civic interiors, and the overlap between home and city

Library Threshold Study explores the spaces between street and reading room, asking how arrivals, sightlines, and material cues can make knowledge spaces feel open to a wider range of users.

Question

Many libraries are generous in program but hesitant in atmosphere. Their entrances can feel overly controlled, and the transition from outside to inside often signals expertise before welcome.

This study reframes the threshold as the key civic moment. What happens in the first thirty seconds determines whether a person feels entitled to stay.

Findings

Low shelves, visible staff points, warm lighting, and a sequence of half-public rooms make the building easier to read. When readers can understand the space immediately, formal signage does less of the work.

The proposal also brings domestic cues into civic space: shared tables, softer acoustics, and edges where one can sit without already committing to a long stay.

Implication

The project is less about a single building than about a principle: civic institutions should communicate welcome spatially, not only verbally.

In that sense, the threshold is not a leftover zone. It is where public trust begins.
