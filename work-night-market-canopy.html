Project / Culture + Public Space

Night Market Canopy

Location

Busan, Korea

Timeline

2026

Focus

Safer evening streets, vendor infrastructure, and low-cost public comfort

Night Market Canopy reclaims an overly vehicle-focused corridor by giving temporary vendors durable support: power, shade, storage, and a clearer public identity after dark.

The brief

The district already had informal evening activity, but stalls were scattered, seating was inconsistent, and circulation often conflicted with traffic. The project asked how a night market could feel safer and more generous without losing its improvised energy.

The answer was not a permanent hall but a repeatable canopy system paired with power hookups, washable surfaces, and simple street furniture that could be rearranged as demand changed.

Spatial strategy

The proposal widens the pedestrian zone at key crossings, inserts weather-protected stall bays, and treats seating as part of the vendor landscape rather than as separate furniture. That keeps the market readable even when it is only half occupied.

A layered lighting scheme differentiates movement routes from places of pause. This reduces conflict, improves safety, and makes the corridor feel active at multiple scales.

Outcome

Night Market Canopy shows how modest infrastructure can materially improve public life. By focusing on comfort, storage, and legibility, the design gives informal commerce a more durable place in the city.

It is a public space project, but also a cultural one: the market is understood not simply as retail, but as an evening ritual that gives the street a social identity.
