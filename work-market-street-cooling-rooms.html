Project / Public Space + Culture

Market Street Cooling Rooms

Location

Amman, Jordan

Timeline

2022

Focus

Heat relief, public health, and small-scale upgrades for active retail streets

Market Street Cooling Rooms inserts small climate shelters into a dense commercial corridor, creating places to recover, drink water, and pause without stepping away from everyday street life.

Problem

The street was vibrant but physically demanding during summer peaks. Shopkeepers improvised shade and water access where they could, yet there was no coordinated public strategy for rest or heat relief.

The project explores how a network of compact cooling rooms could support vendors, older adults, delivery workers, and families moving through the district.

Proposal

Each cooling room is small enough to fit within existing building setbacks or reused storefront fragments. Together, they form a distributed public support system rather than one centralized facility.

Outside, shaded stoops, awnings, and refill stations extend their usefulness into the street. The design assumes people will move in and out quickly, so visibility and ease of access matter as much as temperature control.

Lesson

The project treats public health as a spatial issue. Relief should be close, recognizable, and integrated into ordinary life rather than hidden behind institutions.

By keeping each intervention modest, the street can improve incrementally while preserving its existing commercial culture.
