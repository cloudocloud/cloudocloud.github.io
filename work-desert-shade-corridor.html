Project / Public Space + Mobility

Desert Shade Corridor

Location

Doha, Qatar

Timeline

2024

Focus

Walkability in extreme heat, shaded links, and everyday local access

Desert Shade Corridor tests how stations, schools, and neighborhood retail can be linked through a climate-responsive walking system that prioritizes comfort in extreme heat.

Starting point

Many daily destinations were geographically close but functionally far apart because walking between them felt exposed and exhausting. The project reframes the gap between buildings as the real site of intervention.

Instead of relying on one large enclosed structure, the design builds a chain of smaller supports: arcades, planted buffers, drinking points, misting moments, and cooled rest bays.

Toolkit

The corridor is assembled from repeatable elements that can adapt to different street widths and funding conditions. Materials prioritize low glare, shadow depth, and durability under heavy sun exposure.

Equally important is the timing of use. The route is designed to remain useful in early morning and evening, when pedestrian life can expand rapidly if comfort is visible and reliable.

Takeaway

The corridor expands the definition of mobility by focusing on bodily comfort. If people cannot arrive on foot without strain, the network is incomplete no matter how short the distance appears on a map.

Here, climate adaptation is not hidden infrastructure. It becomes a visible invitation to move differently through the city.
