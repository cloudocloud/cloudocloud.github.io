Project / Mobility + Waterfront

Riverside Mobility Garden

Location

Portland, USA

Timeline

2024

Focus

Flood adaptation, bike movement, and continuous public landscape

Riverside Mobility Garden proposes a continuous river edge where cycle movement, stormwater retention, and everyday resting space are layered into one legible public system.

Framework

The corridor was already heavily used for commuting, but its river relationship was weak and its flood response mostly hidden in engineering infrastructure. The project reimagines the edge as a visible landscape of retention gardens, shared paths, and stepped sitting areas.

By turning resilience measures into public amenities, the design makes environmental adaptation understandable at ground level.

Design moves

The proposal separates faster bike flow from slower walking routes while preserving visual contact between them. Planting bands, seating decks, and rain gardens act as subtle buffers rather than barriers.

Lighting and route markers are intentionally minimal so the corridor remains calm at dusk. The emphasis is on readability through section, not signage.

Impact

Riverside Mobility Garden suggests that infrastructure projects should be evaluated not only by throughput, but by whether they make room for slower, more social, and more climate-aware ways of moving.

The river becomes less of a backdrop and more of a daily companion, which changes how the whole district is perceived.
