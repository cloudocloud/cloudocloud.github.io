Project / Mobility + Public Space

Tram Square Reframed

Location

Vienna, Austria

Timeline

2025

Focus

Safer crossings, visible waiting, and a square that reads as civic space first

Tram Square Reframed rebalances a busy interchange by simplifying movement lines, reducing conflict points, and enlarging the places where people actually spend time waiting.

Challenge

The square worked operationally but felt fragmented. Passengers crossed in diagonal bursts, seating was either too exposed or too far from movement, and the tram stop lacked the civic quality expected from such a central address.

The project focused on clarifying how the square is read at first glance: where to cross, where to queue, and where to pause without blocking others.

Approach

The redesign widens median platforms, introduces stronger weather protection, and compresses vehicular turning movements to free up space for planted edges and benches. Paving cues and lighting are used to reinforce the hierarchy of routes without relying on excessive signage.

Importantly, the proposal treats waiting as an experience rather than a leftover. Shade, sitting, and sightlines are designed into the interchange from the beginning.

Value

The project demonstrates that mobility upgrades are also public space upgrades. Better transfers do not only save time; they change whether the square feels stressful, anonymous, or genuinely civic.

That broader lens is what links the project to the Gehl-style reference: transport is evaluated through comfort, invitation, and the quality of the street-level experience.
